# Baseline Temporal Reasoning Dataset Sample

A qualitative sample drawn directly from the 500 records generated by `generator.js` during our baseline evaluation. These records demonstrate the "format jitter," "multi-unit cascade," and our base-60 `[THINK]` block reasoning structure that we use for PoT.

## Record 1: Base-10 Shadow Pair
Immediately preceding temporal problems, the system occasionally asks the model to perform raw base-10 addition. This contrastive learning teaches the model when to use base-60 vs base-10.
```text
What is 54 + 60?
[THINK] 54+60=114. [RESULT] 114
```

## Record 2: Day Rollover (Midnight Crossing)
A standard domestic scenario testing if the model correctly rolls over `24:00` to `00:00`.
```text
I put the roast in the oven at 23.54. It needs 60 minutes. When is it done?
[THINK] M_start = 54. 54 + 60 = 114. 114 >= 60? Yes. 114 - 60 = 54. Carry 1 to Hours. H_start = 23. 23 + 1 = 24. 24 >= 24? Yes. 24 - 24 = 00. Day rollover. Format hour: 00. [RESULT] 00:54
[ANSWER] 00:54 [/ANSWER]
```

## Record 3: Triple-Carry Cascade
Testing a large minute addition (155 minutes = 2 hours and 35 minutes) requiring a carry of `3` to the hours unit. Notice the formatting logic forcing `00` for the minute placeholder.
```text
The train departs at 16:25. The journey is 155 minutes. Arrival time?
[THINK] M_start = 25. 25 + 155 = 180. 180 >= 60? Yes. 180 - 180 = 00. Carry 3 to Hours. H_start = 16. 16 + 3 = 19. Hours = 19. Format minute: 00. [RESULT] 19:00
[ANSWER] 19:00 [/ANSWER]
```

## Record 4: Logistics Scenario
A classic business scheduling math problem requiring subtraction modulo 60 (173 - 120 = 53).
```text
The meeting starts at 15:10 and lasts 163 minutes. When does it end?
[THINK] M_start = 10. 10 + 163 = 173. 173 >= 60? Yes. 173 - 120 = 53. Carry 2 to Hours. H_start = 15. 15 + 2 = 17. Hours = 17. [RESULT] 17:53
[ANSWER] 17:53 [/ANSWER]
```

## Record 5: Zero-Padding Formatting
Ensuring the output maintains the strict `09:18` rather than just `9:18`.
```text
Take your medicine at 6:32. The next dose is in 166 minutes. When?
[THINK] M_start = 32. 32 + 166 = 198. 198 >= 60? Yes. 198 - 180 = 18. Carry 3 to Hours. H_start = 6. 6 + 3 = 9. Hours = 09. Format hour: 09. [RESULT] 09:18
[ANSWER] 09:18 [/ANSWER]
```
