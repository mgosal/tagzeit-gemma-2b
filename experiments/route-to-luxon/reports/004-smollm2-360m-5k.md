# Experiment 004: SmolLM2-360M — 5,000 Step Format Soak

**Date:** 2026-03-30
**Status:** In Progress ⏳
**Model:** `HuggingFaceTB/SmolLM2-360M-Instruct` (362M parameters)
**Architecture:** Route-to-Luxon (ADR-001)
**Method:** Full fine-tune (no LoRA)
**Predecessor:** Experiment 003 (500 steps, 12.5% E2E accuracy)

## Objective

Test whether increasing the training duration from 500 steps to 5,000 steps solves the "Format/Bracket Dropping" phenomenon (`Engine error: Expected 6 tokens for ROUTE_TIME_ADD, got 5`) that crippled the E2E score in Exp 003 despite theoretically high vocabulary accuracy.

## Hypothesis

At 500 steps, the model learns the 104 domain tokens but fails to reliably structure them into the strict sequential grammar required by the deterministic Luxon engine. By soaking the model 10x longer on the formatting task, its output structure should ossify, dramatically decreasing Engine syntax errors and raising E2E eval scores closer to raw token accuracy.
*Note: Due to our single-step training data, we also hypothesize it may struggle on multi-step/compositional generalization tests until further training data covers chaining.*

## Results

**Status:** Completed ✅
**E2E Accuracy:** 12/48 (25.0%) — *Increase from 12.5% in Exp 003*

| Category | Score | Notes |
|:---------|:------|:------|
| Standard | 83% | Strong baseline performance. |
| Format Robust | 40% | Improving but still sensitive to spacing. |
| Minute Carry | 25% | Struggles with arithmetic precision. |
| Subtraction | 0% | **Logical Error:** Model frequently maps "ago" to `[ROUTE_TIME_ADD]`. |
| Cascade/Rollover| 0% | Model generates valid routes but with incorrect duration arguments (e.g., 1 min → 8 min). |

### Qualitative Analysis

The 5,000-step "soak" was highly successful in its primary objective: **Grammatical Ossification**.
- In Experiment 003 (500 steps), the model suffered from "Bracket Dropping" and syntax errors.
- In Experiment 004, the model **consistently generates syntactically perfect routing calls**. Every single test case now produces valid domain tokens.
- **Problem:** While the *structure* is fixed, the *symbolic mapping* is noisy. The model hallucinations duration arguments (mapping "1 minute" to `[ARG_MIN_07]`) and logical directions (mapping "before" to `[ROUTE_TIME_ADD]`).

## Conclusions

1. **Duration Hallucination:** The model has learned the "shape" of the answer but not the mapping between literal numbers ("1 minute") and their domain-typed equivalents (`[ARG_MIN_01]`). This suggests a need for more diverse duration data or a second stage focusing on the "Number-to-Token" mapping.
2. **Logic Flip:** The persistent mapping of subtraction to addition suggests the `[ROUTE_TIME_SUB]` token has low salience or the training data for subtraction is under-represented compared to the addition baseline.
3. **Data Chaining:** As hypothesized, multi-step reasoning (Cascades) remains at 0% due to the single-step nature of the current training set.
